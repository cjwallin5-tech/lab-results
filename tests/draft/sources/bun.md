BUN measures urea nitrogen, a waste product from protein breakdown that the kidneys
normally filter out. The test helps detect kidney problems early, when treatment is most
effective.

**Lower than normal:** can indicate not enough protein in the diet, malnutrition, or
liver disease.

**Higher than normal:** often suggests the kidneys aren't filtering as well as they
should, but can also come from dehydration, burns, certain medications, a high-protein
diet, or a recent heart attack. The source notes an abnormal result doesn't always mean
a treatable condition — normal ranges also vary by age and sex.
