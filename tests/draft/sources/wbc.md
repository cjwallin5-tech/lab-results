A white blood count measures the number of white blood cells (part of the immune
system) in the blood. It's most often used to help diagnose or monitor disorders
related to having a high or low white blood cell count.

**Lower than normal (leukopenia):** can result from bone marrow issues (from infection
or chemotherapy), cancers affecting the bone marrow, autoimmune disorders like lupus, or
HIV.

**Higher than normal (leukocytosis):** may indicate infection, inflammatory disease,
allergies, leukemia, or tissue damage (burns, surgery) — and can also come from smoking,
stress, a reaction to medication, or pregnancy.
