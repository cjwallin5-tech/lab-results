A platelet count measures the number of platelets in the blood. It's used to monitor or
diagnose conditions involving too much bleeding or clotting, and is often part of a
routine complete blood count.

**Lower than normal (thrombocytopenia):** may be linked to blood cancers like leukemia,
viral infections such as hepatitis, autoimmune disease, bone marrow damage, cirrhosis,
vitamin B12 deficiency, or a temporary drop during pregnancy. Can lead to longer
bleeding after minor injuries.

**Higher than normal (thrombocytosis):** may be linked to certain cancers, anemia,
inflammatory bowel disease, rheumatoid arthritis, infection, or recovering from
significant blood loss. Carries a risk of blood clots.
