Hematocrit is the percent of blood made up of red blood cells. It's commonly included in
routine checkups and helps diagnose blood disorders like anemia or polycythemia.

**Lower than normal:** can indicate recent or chronic blood loss, not enough red blood
cells (anemia), or conditions affecting the bone marrow.

**Higher than normal:** may suggest the body is producing too many red blood cells (due
to lung disease, heart conditions, or polycythemia), or that the fluid (plasma) portion
of blood is low from dehydration or shock. Living at high altitude can also raise levels.
