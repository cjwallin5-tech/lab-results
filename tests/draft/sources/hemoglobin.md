Hemoglobin is an iron-rich protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from the lungs
to the rest of the body. The test is commonly used to screen for anemia and assess red
blood cell health.

**Lower than normal:** may be a sign of anemia (several types), iron deficiency, liver
disease, cancer, blood loss, or a lack of nutrients like iron or vitamin B-12. The source
emphasizes these are things a low result "may be a sign of," not a diagnosis on their own.

**Higher than normal:** may be a sign of lung disease, heart disease, polycythemia vera
(the body making too many red blood cells), or sleep apnea. Altitude, diet, activity
level, dehydration, smoking, and certain medications can also affect the result.
