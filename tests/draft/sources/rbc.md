An RBC count measures the number of red blood cells in the blood — the cells that carry
hemoglobin and oxygen throughout the body. It's typically part of a complete blood count
and helps detect red blood cell disorders, including anemia, before symptoms appear.

**Lower than normal:** may indicate anemia, leukemia, malnutrition, multiple myeloma,
kidney failure, blood loss, alcohol use disorder, or pregnancy.

**Higher than normal:** can result from dehydration, heart disease, polycythemia vera,
lung scarring or lung disease, kidney cancer, sleep apnea, anabolic steroid use, or
living at high altitude.
