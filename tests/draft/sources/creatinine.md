Creatinine is a waste product made as muscles break down, normally filtered out by the
kidneys. The test checks how well the kidneys are working.

**Lower than normal:** can indicate malnutrition, or a condition that shrinks muscle
mass (long illness, a nerve disorder, or age-related muscle loss). Serious liver disease
can also lower it, though a low result is uncommon.

**Higher than normal:** may suggest kidney disease or injury — including infection, poor
blood flow to the kidneys, a urinary blockage, or kidney failure. It can also come from
dehydration, muscle disorders or injury, muscular dystrophy, intense exercise, or a
diet high in meat — not necessarily a kidney problem.
