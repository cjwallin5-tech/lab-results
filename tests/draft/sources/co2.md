This test measures the amount of carbon dioxide in the blood, mostly in the form of
bicarbonate, an electrolyte. It helps diagnose or monitor electrolyte imbalances
affecting the kidneys, lungs, or liver.

**Lower than normal:** may signal Addison disease, diabetic ketoacidosis, shock,
metabolic acidosis (from kidney/liver disease or ongoing diarrhea), or respiratory
alkalosis from hyperventilation or lung problems.

**Higher than normal:** can indicate lung disease, Cushing's syndrome, kidney failure,
or metabolic alkalosis from vomiting, dehydration, or an eating disorder. The source
stresses an abnormal result "doesn't necessarily mean you have a medical condition that
needs treatment" without further testing.
