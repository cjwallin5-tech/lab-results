Chloride is an electrolyte that helps regulate body fluids and acid-base balance. It's
usually ordered as part of routine screening or when checking for kidney disease, heart
failure, or symptoms of fluid imbalance like persistent vomiting, diarrhea, or
dehydration.

**Lower than normal:** may indicate heart failure, lung disease, Addison disease
(affecting adrenal hormones), or metabolic alkalosis (too much base in the blood),
which can cause muscle twitching or tingling.

**Higher than normal:** may signal dehydration, kidney disease, or metabolic acidosis
(too much acid in the blood), which can cause nausea, vomiting, and fatigue. The source
notes an abnormal result doesn't always mean a medical problem — medications and fluid
loss can also affect it.
