A calcium blood test checks calcium levels as part of general health screening and to
help diagnose conditions affecting the bones, kidneys, digestion, thyroid, and
parathyroid glands. Calcium is important for nerve, muscle, and heart function.

**Lower than normal (hypocalcemia):** can indicate low blood protein (from liver disease
or malnutrition), underactive parathyroid glands, not enough calcium/vitamin D/magnesium
in the diet, pancreatitis, or kidney disease.

**Higher than normal (hypercalcemia):** may signal overactive parathyroid glands,
certain cancers (including cancer that has spread to bone), bone disorders like Paget's
disease, or taking too much vitamin D over time.
