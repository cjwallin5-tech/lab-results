Glucose is a sugar that is the body's main source of energy. The test screens for
prediabetes and diabetes, helps diagnose symptoms of abnormal glucose levels, and
monitors some medications' side effects.

**Lower than normal:** uncommon in people without diabetes; when it happens, possible
causes include liver or kidney disease, underactive adrenal glands, pituitary disorders,
hypothyroidism, malnutrition, certain medications, or alcohol use disorder.

**Higher than normal:** may mean you have or are at risk for diabetes. Other possible
causes include an overactive thyroid, pancreas problems, stress from surgery or serious
illness, adrenal gland disorders, or certain medications.
