Potassium is an electrolyte that helps control fluid levels and the body's acid-base
balance. It's checked during routine exams or to monitor conditions like diabetes,
kidney disease, and heart disease.

**Lower than normal (hypokalemia):** may be linked to diuretic medications, fluid loss
from diarrhea or vomiting, excessive laxative use, adrenal gland disorders, kidney
disease, alcohol use disorder, or not getting enough potassium in the diet. Can cause
irregular heartbeat, muscle cramps, and weakness.

**Higher than normal (hyperkalemia):** often relates to kidney disease, Addison disease,
injury or burns, uncontrolled diabetes, certain medications, or eating a lot of
potassium-rich foods. Can cause abnormal heart rhythms, fatigue, muscle weakness, or
numbness/tingling.
