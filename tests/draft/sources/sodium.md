Sodium is an electrolyte that helps nerves and muscles work properly. The test checks
whether sodium is in a healthy range, since abnormal levels can point to kidney
problems, dehydration, or other conditions.

**Lower than normal:** may signal sodium loss from diarrhea or vomiting, or a condition
causing the body to hold onto fluid — kidney disease, liver cirrhosis, heart failure,
certain brain or lung conditions, or some medications (including pain relievers and
antidepressants).

**Higher than normal:** can indicate dehydration from not drinking enough fluid or from
diarrhea, adrenal gland disorders, kidney disease, or diabetes insipidus. Without
treatment, very high levels can become serious.
